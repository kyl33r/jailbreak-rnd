# JailbreakRadar: Comprehensive Assessment

## Metadata

- **Title:** JailbreakRadar: Comprehensive Assessment of Jailbreak Attacks Against LLMs
- **Authors:** Junjie Chu, Yugeng Liu, Ziqing Yang, Xinyue Shen, Michael Backes, Yang Zhang
- **ArXiv:** arXiv:2402.05668
- **Date:** February 2024 (Updated May 2025)
- **Type:** Survey/Assessment Paper
- **Venue:** ACL 2025

## Summary

Provides a systematic taxonomy and comprehensive assessment of existing jailbreak attacks. Evaluates attack effectiveness across multiple models and settings, finding significant risks even in the latest LLMs.

## Key Insights

1. **Systematic Taxonomy**: Comprehensive classification of jailbreak attack methods.

2. **Real-World Black-Box Risk**: Significant jailbreak risks in realistic black-box attack scenarios.

3. **Static Policy Limitations**: Static policies and questions are insufficient for defense.

4. **Latest Model Vulnerability**: Even the most recent LLMs face significant jailbreak risks.

## Research Methodology

### Taxonomy Dimensions

1. **Attack Type**: Manual vs. automated
2. **Model Access**: White-box vs. black-box
3. **Target Model**: Open-source vs. closed-source
4. **Query Format**: Single-turn vs. multi-turn

## Key Findings

### Attack Effectiveness

- In realistic black-box settings, jailbreak attacks pose substantial security threats
- Attack success rates remain high across model generations

### Defense Challenges

- Current defenses are insufficient
- Static approaches fail against adaptive attacks
- Need for dynamic, adaptive defense mechanisms

## Research Contributions

1. **Comprehensive Taxonomy**: Systematic classification
2. **Unified Evaluation**: Standardized assessment framework
3. **Empirical Analysis**: Large-scale empirical study across models
4. **Real-World Relevance**: Focus on practical black-box scenarios

## Dataset

- Uses harmful question datasets
- Incorporates policies from multiple companies
- Tests against diverse attack vectors

## Key Implication

Jailbreak attacks are not merely theoretical concerns but practical security threats requiring ongoing research and adaptive defenses.
